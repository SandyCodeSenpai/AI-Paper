# LLaMA: Open and Efficient Foundation Language Models

## TL;DR

LLaMA is a family of decoder-only Transformer LMs (7B / 13B / 33B / 65B) trained by Meta on 1–1.4T tokens of *only public* data. By training smaller models past Chinchilla-optimal token counts, LLaMA-13B beats GPT-3 (175B) on most benchmarks and LLaMA-65B competes with Chinchilla-70B and PaLM-540B. Weights released to research community — this paper kicked off the modern open-weight LLM era and locked in the architectural recipe everyone now iterates on (pre-norm + RMSNorm + SwiGLU + RoPE).

## Problem & Motivation

- **Inference, not training, dominates serving cost.** Chinchilla (Hoffmann 2022) showed compute-optimal training favors smaller models on more data (~20 tokens/param). LLaMA pushes further: train *past* compute-optimal so the resulting smaller model is cheap at inference.
- **Reproducibility / openness.** GPT-3, Chinchilla, PaLM trained on undocumented or proprietary data ("Books – 2TB", "social media"). LLaMA constrains itself to only publicly available datasets so weights can be released openly.
- Authors explicitly observe: "the performance of a 7B model continues to improve even after 1T tokens" — well past Chinchilla's recommendation.

## Background / Prior Work

- Kaplan 2020 — early scaling laws, more params = better
- [[concepts/chinchilla-scaling]] — Hoffmann 2022 refined this for training compute
- GPT-3, Gopher, PaLM — closed-data, closed-weights peers
- OPT, BLOOM, GPT-NeoX, GLM — earlier open-weights but not competitive with closed SOTA
- Architectural ingredients borrowed from: GPT-3 (pre-norm), PaLM (SwiGLU), GPT-NeoX (RoPE)

## Key Contribution

1. A new state-of-the-art among open-weight LLMs at the time of release.
2. Demonstrated that public-only data suffices to reach SOTA quality.
3. Established the **canonical decoder-only architecture stack** (pre-norm + RMSNorm + SwiGLU + RoPE) that all subsequent open LLMs use.
4. Released weights for 7B/13B/33B/65B for research, seeding the open ecosystem.

## Method

### Pretraining data (Table 1, 1.4T tokens after BPE tokenization)

| Source | Sampling | Epochs | Disk |
|---|---|---|---|
| English CommonCrawl | 67.0% | 1.10 | 3.3 TB |
| C4 | 15.0% | 1.06 | 783 GB |
| GitHub | 4.5% | 0.64 | 328 GB |
| Wikipedia (20 langs) | 4.5% | 2.45 | 83 GB |
| Books (Gutenberg + Books3) | 4.5% | 2.23 | 85 GB |
| ArXiv | 2.5% | 1.06 | 92 GB |
| StackExchange | 2.0% | 1.03 | 78 GB |

CCNet pipeline for CommonCrawl: line-level dedup, fastText English filter, KenLM low-quality filter, plus a fastText classifier trained to discriminate Wikipedia-referenced pages from random pages (quality signal).

Tokenizer: SentencePiece BPE. Numbers split into individual digits; bytes-fallback for unknown UTF-8.

### Architecture — Transformer with three modifications

Decoder-only Transformer (Vaswani 2017) with:

- **[[concepts/pre-normalization]] + [[concepts/rmsnorm]]** (from GPT-3 / Zhang & Sennrich 2019) — normalize the input of each sublayer, not the output; use RMSNorm instead of LayerNorm.
- **[[concepts/swiglu]]** (from PaLM / Shazeer 2020) — replaces ReLU in FFN. Uses hidden dim (2/3) × 4d to keep param count comparable.
- **[[concepts/rope]]** (from GPT-NeoX / Su 2021) — rotary positional embeddings injected at every attention layer; no absolute position embeddings.

| Model | dim | heads | layers | lr | tokens |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7B | 4096 | 32 | 32 | 3e-4 | 1.0T |
| 13B | 5120 | 40 | 40 | 3e-4 | 1.0T |
| 33B | 6656 | 52 | 60 | 1.5e-4 | 1.4T |
| 65B | 8192 | 64 | 80 | 1.5e-4 | 1.4T |

All models: 4M-token batches, 2048-token context.

### Optimizer

- AdamW, β₁=0.9, **β₂=0.95** (lower than usual — stability at scale)
- Cosine LR schedule, final LR = 10% of peak
- Weight decay 0.1, grad clip 1.0, 2000 warmup steps

### Efficient implementation

- xformers memory-efficient causal attention (Rabe & Staats 2021): no materialized attention matrix; skips Q·K computations for masked positions
- Manual backward pass for transformer layers (skip PyTorch autograd for the expensive parts) + selective activation checkpointing
- Model + sequence parallelism (Korthikanti 2022)
- Overlap of compute with `all_reduce` communication
- LLaMA-65B: ~380 tokens/sec/GPU on 2048 A100-80GB → 1.4T tokens in ~21 days

## Results

### Headline numbers

- **LLaMA-13B beats GPT-3 (175B) on most benchmarks**, despite being 10× smaller.
- **LLaMA-65B competitive with Chinchilla-70B and PaLM-540B** on common-sense reasoning, reading comprehension, code, and math (notably beats Minerva-62B on GSM8k without math-specific training).
- **LLaMA-65B is SOTA on NaturalQuestions and TriviaQA** in the zero/few-shot setting; runs on a single V100.

### Where LLaMA falls behind

- **MMLU:** LLaMA-65B 63.4 vs Chinchilla-70B 67.5, PaLM-540B 69.3. Authors hypothesize cause: their pretraining has only ~177GB books/papers vs up to 2TB in peer corpora. Books carry knowledge density.

### Instruction finetuning (brief, §4)

LLaMA-65B → LLaMA-I via Chung 2022 protocol → MMLU 63.4 → 68.9. Confirms instruction tuning is a cheap further win; explicitly out of scope for the paper.

### Bias / Toxicity

- **RealToxicityPrompts:** toxicity *increases* with model size (0.106 → 0.128 from 7B → 65B). "Respectful" framing helps small models, *hurts* 65B (0.141). [Concerning.]
- **CrowS-Pairs:** LLaMA-65B avg 66.6 (vs GPT-3 67.2, OPT 69.5). Worse than peers on religion (+10% over OPT), age, gender.
- **WinoGender:** strong gender-occupation bias on "gotcha" pronouns.
- **TruthfulQA:** 65B truthful 57% / truthful*informative 53% — better than GPT-3 but still frequently hallucinates.

### Carbon footprint (§6, Table 15)

- LLaMA-65B alone: 1,022,362 A100-hours, 449 MWh, **173 tCO₂eq**
- All four models combined: 2,638 MWh, **1,015 tCO₂eq**
- Comparable to OPT-175B (137 tCO₂eq) and BLOOM-175B (183 tCO₂eq) per model.

## Limitations

Authors acknowledge:
- No instruction tuning / RLHF — base model only
- Bias and toxicity scale with model size; not solved
- TruthfulQA shows substantial hallucination
- Pretraining mix may underweight books/papers (MMLU gap)
- Dataset filtering doesn't fully scrub bias-laden web content

Not stated but worth noting:
- 2048-token context — short by modern standards
- Multilingual coverage limited to 20 European languages (no CJK, Arabic, Hindi)
- Dense attention only — no GQA, no sliding window, no MoE
- License was research-only at release (commercial use waited for Llama 2)

## My Takeaways

- The architectural recipe in §2.2 — pre-norm + RMSNorm + SwiGLU + RoPE — is the most influential single section of this paper. Almost every subsequent open LLM uses it.
- Chinchilla showed *compute-optimal*. LLaMA reframed the question to *inference-optimal* — a much more practically useful objective for anyone running models at serving time.
- The MMLU gap is a clean signal that data composition (esp. high-quality books and papers) matters as much as token count for knowledge-heavy benchmarks.
- The toxicity-rises-with-scale finding is a quiet warning that subsequent papers (Llama 2 onward) had to address with safety training.

## Open Questions

- How much of the LLaMA-vs-Chinchilla advantage is from training duration vs from architecture choices vs from data quality? Paper doesn't ablate.
- Why does "respectful" prompting *hurt* 65B on RealToxicityPrompts? Does scale change the model's interpretation of the framing prompt?
- What's the marginal gain of each architectural change individually? No ablations of pre-norm vs SwiGLU vs RoPE here.

## Glossary

- **Decoder-only Transformer:** Transformer using only the autoregressive decoder stack (no encoder); each token attends only to previous tokens. Standard for modern LLMs.
- **BPE / SentencePiece:** Byte-pair encoding subword tokenizer; SentencePiece is Google's implementation.
- **Pre-normalization:** Applying LayerNorm/RMSNorm to the input of each sublayer instead of after the residual addition. Improves stability at depth.
- **RMSNorm:** Layer normalization that drops mean-centering and bias; only rescales by root-mean-square. Cheaper, equally effective at scale.
- **SwiGLU:** Gated activation `Swish(xW) ⊙ (xV)` in the FFN; replaces ReLU.
- **RoPE (Rotary Positional Embedding):** Inject position by rotating Q/K vectors by an angle proportional to position; gives relative-position-aware attention without explicit position embeddings.
- **Compute-optimal (Chinchilla):** Allocation of params and tokens that minimizes loss for a fixed training-FLOP budget; found ~20 tokens per param.
- **Activation checkpointing:** Recomputing forward activations during backward to save memory at the cost of compute.
- **Model parallelism:** Splitting model parameters across devices (tensor parallelism splits matrices; pipeline parallelism splits layers).
- **xformers:** Meta's library of memory-efficient attention kernels.
